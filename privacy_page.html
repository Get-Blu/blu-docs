Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 23, 2026

Data Collection

blu does not collect, store, or transmit your source code to our servers. All workspace indexing happens locally on your machine. We do not have access to your codebase, project files, or any code you write or edit using blu. Your intellectual property remains entirely yours, and we have no visibility into it.

API Key Storage

When you provide API keys for language model providers (Anthropic, OpenAI, etc.), these keys are stored locally in VS Code's secure credential storage system. We never transmit your API keys to blu servers. All API requests go directly from your machine to the provider you've selected. You maintain full control over your API credentials at all times.

Anonymous Usage Analytics

With your explicit consent, we may collect anonymous usage statistics such as feature usage patterns, error logs, and performance metrics. This data is completely anonymized and contains no personally identifiable information or code snippets. Analytics help us understand how developers use blu so we can improve the extension. You can opt out at any time in the extension settings, and opting out does not affect functionality.

Third-Party Services

When you use blu with cloud-based AI providers, your code snippets and prompts are sent directly to those providers according to their respective privacy policies. We act only as a pass-through—your data goes from your machine to the provider without touching our servers. We strongly recommend reviewing the data retention and privacy policies of Anthropic, OpenAI, or any other provider you choose to use. Many providers offer zero-retention options for enterprise customers.

Local-Only Mode

When using blu with local models via Ollama or similar solutions, all data remains on your machine. No internet connection is required, and no data leaves your device. This mode is ideal for working with sensitive codebases, proprietary projects, or in air-gapped environments where security is paramount.

Open Source Transparency

As an open-source project, our entire codebase is publicly available on GitHub. You can verify our privacy practices by reviewing the source code yourself or having your security team audit it. We believe transparency is the best form of privacy assurance. If you discover any privacy concerns in our code, please report them immediately through our GitHub security policy.

Updates to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for legal compliance. When we make significant changes, we'll notify users through the extension and update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of blu after policy updates constitutes acceptance of the changes.

Contact

For privacy-related questions, concerns, or data requests, please contact us on our GitHub repository. We take privacy seriously and will respond to all inquiries within 48 hours.
